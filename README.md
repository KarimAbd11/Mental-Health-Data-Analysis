# Mental-Health-Data-Analysis

<h3>Overview</h3>
<p>This project explores mental health trends using survey data collected from participants across multiple countries. The analysis focuses on demographic patterns, treatment-seeking behavior, workplace-related mental health impacts, and family history influences. The project demonstrates data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), visualization, and interpretation using Python.</p>

<h3>Objectives</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Explore and clean survey data</li>
  <li>Analyze treatment-seeking patterns</li>
  <li>Investigate relationships between stress, family history, and workplace interference</li>
  <li>Create visualizations to communicate findings effectively</li>
</ul>

<h3>Tools & Libraries</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Python</li>
  <li>Pandas</li>
  <li>Matplotlib</li>
  <li>Plotly</li>
  <li>Google Colab</li>
</ul>

<h3>Dataset</h3>
<p>Mental health dataset, obtained from Kaggle, containing a variety of features mental health, workplace experiences, treatment-seeking behavior, and demographic information.</p>

<h3>Key Findings</h3>
<p>The participant pool was predominantly male.</p>
<p>Most respondents were located in North America.</p>
<p>Participants with a family history of mental health conditions were more likely to seek treatment.</p>
<p>Mental health challenges appeared associated with workplace interference.</p>
<p>Several demographic imbalances may affect dataset representativeness.</p>

<h3>Limitations</h3>
<p>Dataset contains self-reported responses.</p>
<p>Gender imbalance may affect representativeness.</p>
<p>Most participants originated from a limited geographic region.</p>
<p>Observed relationships are correlational rather than causal.</p>

<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>This project demonstrates the application of data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and visualization techniques to derive meaningful insights from mental health survey data. The findings highlight important trends while also emphasizing the importance of considering dataset limitations in public health research.</p>
